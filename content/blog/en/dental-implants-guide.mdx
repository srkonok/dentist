Dental implants are now the gold standard for replacing missing teeth. Unlike dentures or bridges, implants function and feel almost exactly like natural teeth. Here is what you need to know.

## What Is a Dental Implant?

A dental implant has three parts:

1. **The implant post** — a small titanium screw placed into the jawbone, which fuses with the bone over time (a process called osseointegration).
2. **The abutment** — a connector piece that sits on top of the post once it has integrated.
3. **The crown** — a custom-made ceramic tooth that attaches to the abutment and looks exactly like a natural tooth.

## Who Is a Candidate?

You may be a good candidate if:

- You have one or more missing teeth
- Your jawbone is fully developed and has sufficient density
- You do not have uncontrolled diabetes or other conditions that affect healing
- You are a non-smoker (or willing to quit — smoking significantly affects success rates)

## The Procedure Step by Step

1. **Consultation and imaging** — We take X-rays (and sometimes a 3D CT scan) to assess your bone.
2. **Implant placement** — Performed under local anaesthesia. You should feel no pain, only slight pressure.
3. **Healing period** — 3–6 months for the implant to fuse with the bone.
4. **Abutment and crown fitting** — Once healed, the final tooth is placed — usually in two short appointments.

## Caring for Your Implant

Implants require the same care as natural teeth: brush twice daily, floss, and attend regular check-ups. With proper care, implants can last a lifetime.
