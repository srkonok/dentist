Having a tooth extracted can feel daunting, but with the right aftercare your recovery should be smooth and quick. Here are the most important steps to follow in the days after your procedure.

## The First 24 Hours

The first day is the most critical. Follow these guidelines carefully:

- **Keep the gauze in place** for at least 30–45 minutes after leaving the clinic to allow a blood clot to form.
- **Avoid spitting or rinsing vigorously** — this can dislodge the clot and cause a painful condition called "dry socket."
- **No straws** — the suction motion has the same effect.
- **Rest** and keep your head slightly elevated, even while sleeping.
- **Apply an ice pack** to the outside of your cheek for 15 minutes on, 15 minutes off, to reduce swelling.

## What to Eat

Stick to soft foods for the first 48 hours:

- Yoghurt, soup (not too hot), mashed potatoes, scrambled eggs
- Avoid hard, crunchy, or sticky foods
- Avoid very hot or very cold drinks

## Keeping the Area Clean

From day 2 onwards, **gently rinse with warm salt water** (½ teaspoon salt in a glass of warm water) after every meal. This keeps the site clean without disturbing healing tissue.

Brush your teeth normally, but **be very gentle near the extraction site** for the first week.

## Medications

Take any prescribed pain relievers or antibiotics exactly as directed. Do not stop antibiotics early even if you feel better.

## Warning Signs — When to Call Us

Contact the clinic immediately if you notice:

- Severe, worsening pain 3–4 days after extraction (may indicate dry socket)
- Bleeding that won't stop after 1–2 hours of gentle pressure
- Fever, increasing swelling, or pus
