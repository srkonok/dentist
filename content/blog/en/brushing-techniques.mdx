Brushing your teeth sounds simple, but the technique matters more than most people realise. Incorrect brushing can miss plaque, damage gums, or wear down enamel over time.

## Choose the Right Brush

- **Soft bristles** — medium or hard bristles can damage gum tissue and erode enamel.
- **Small head** — a smaller brush head reaches the back molars more easily.
- **Replace every 3 months**, or sooner if the bristles are frayed.
- Electric toothbrushes are excellent — studies show they remove slightly more plaque than manual brushing.

## The Bass Technique (Recommended by Dentists)

1. Hold the brush at a **45-degree angle** to your gum line.
2. Use **small, circular motions** — do not scrub side to side.
3. Work in sections: outer surfaces → inner surfaces → chewing surfaces.
4. Give **2 minutes** total — most people brush for only 45 seconds!
5. Finish by **gently brushing your tongue** to remove bacteria that cause bad breath.

## When to Brush

- **Twice a day** — morning and before bed.
- After breakfast, not immediately before — saliva in the morning provides natural protection.
- Wait **30 minutes after eating acidic foods or drinks** (like orange juice or fizzy drinks) before brushing — acid temporarily softens enamel.

## Don't Forget to Floss

Brushing only cleans about 60% of your tooth surfaces. Floss or an interdental brush removes plaque from between teeth where your toothbrush can't reach. Floss once a day, ideally before brushing at night.

## A Note on Toothpaste

Use a toothpaste containing **fluoride**. Fluoride is the single most important ingredient for preventing tooth decay. The amount of paste needed is only about the size of a pea.
